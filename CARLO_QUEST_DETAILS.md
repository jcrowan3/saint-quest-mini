# Carlo Acutis - Complete Quest Journey

## Overview
Carlo Acutis now has **8 rich, detailed quests** that tell his full story from childhood to his death at age 15. These quests are designed to be relatable and inspiring for 5th-8th graders.

## Quest Progression

### Quest 1: A Normal Kid with an Extraordinary Heart
**Theme:** Introduction to Carlo
**Key Message:** You can love normal things AND put God first
**Story Elements:**
- Born in London, grew up in Milan
- Loved soccer, video games, saxophone, making dog videos
- First Communion at age 7 changed everything
- "The Eucharist is my highway to heaven"
- Shows holiness isn't about being weird or different

**Virtues:** Faith +2, Wisdom +1

---

### Quest 2: One Hour a Week
**Theme:** Self-control and balance with technology
**Key Message:** Be in control of your choices, not controlled by screens
**Story Elements:**
- Got a PlayStation at age 8
- Loved games like Pokémon, Mario, Halo
- Limited himself to 1 hour/week of gaming
- Chose prayer and service over excessive screen time
- Shows wisdom beyond his years

**Virtues:** Wisdom +2, Courage +1

---

### Quest 3: Tupperware and Sleeping Bags
**Theme:** Serving the poor and homeless
**Key Message:** See Jesus in every person, especially the forgotten
**Story Elements:**
- Brought food in Tupperware to homeless people
- Used allowance to buy sleeping bags for those on the streets
- Spent more time serving poor than playing games
- "Jesus is in each one of them"
- Practical, hands-on charity

**Virtues:** Mercy +3, Faith +1

---

### Quest 4: Teaching Himself to Code
**Theme:** Using talents for God's glory
**Key Message:** Learn skills to serve the Kingdom
**Story Elements:**
- Self-taught C and C++ programming
- Saw internet as tool for good or evil
- Wanted to bring people closer to God through technology
- Helped parishes build websites
- Patient and persistent in learning

**Virtues:** Wisdom +2, Faith +1

---

### Quest 5: The Eucharistic Miracles Project
**Theme:** The famous website that made him known worldwide
**Key Message:** Use your gifts to serve God and help others
**Story Elements:**
- Started at age 11
- Researched Eucharistic miracles from around the world
- Built comprehensive website with photos and descriptions
- Months of hard work: researching, coding, designing
- Website spread to thousands of parishes worldwide
- Some people returned to faith because of it

**Virtues:** Wisdom +2, Faith +2

---

### Quest 6: Bad at Soccer, Great at Friendship
**Theme:** Doing things you're not good at for the sake of relationships
**Key Message:** Friendship matters more than being perfect
**Story Elements:**
- Loved soccer but was "quite bad" at it
- Kept playing anyway for friendship and fun
- Never took himself too seriously
- Valued people over performance
- Showed it's okay to not be the best

**Virtues:** Courage +2, Mercy +1

---

### Quest 7: Standing Up for the Bullied
**Theme:** Defending the weak and rejected
**Key Message:** True strength is protecting others
**Story Elements:**
- Saw classmates being bullied and left out
- Stepped in when others were picked on
- Sat with lonely kids at lunch
- Spoke up against teasing
- Taught catechism to younger children
- Treated everyone as precious to God

**Virtues:** Courage +2, Mercy +2

---

### Quest 8: Offering Everything to God
**Theme:** His illness, death, and legacy
**Key Message:** Even suffering has meaning when offered to God
**Story Elements:**
- Diagnosed with leukemia at age 15 (October 2006)
- Offered suffering for the Pope and Church
- Remained joyful despite illness
- "I'm happy to die because I lived my life without wasting even a minute on things that don't please God"
- Died October 12, 2006
- Beatified in 2020
- On track to be first millennial saint

**Virtues:** Faith +3, Courage +2

---

## Total Virtue Points Available
If a student completes all 8 Carlo quests:
- **Faith:** 10 points
- **Wisdom:** 9 points
- **Courage:** 8 points
- **Mercy:** 7 points
- **TOTAL:** 34 virtue points

## What Makes These Quests Special
- **Relatable:** Middle schoolers see themselves in Carlo
- **Detailed:** Each story is 150-200 words with rich narrative
- **Progressive:** Builds from childhood to death
- **Balanced:** Shows Carlo as real person, not perfect
- **Modern:** Addresses technology, gaming, social media age
- **Actionable:** Each quest has clear life lessons
- **Age-appropriate:** Written for 5th-8th grade comprehension
